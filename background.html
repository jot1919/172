Why Childhood Mental Health Matters

Introducing the Problem

According to the American Psychiatric Association, anxiety and depression rates continue to soar in the United States—43% of adults report feeling more anxious than last year. The year prior, it was 37%, and before that, 32%. While these statistics focus on adults, research indicates that stressful life events in adolescence are closely linked to higher anxiety sensitivity, underscoring the importance of addressing mental health needs early in life.

Impact on Youth

Children and teenagers often face academic pressures, social challenges, and emotional changes that can escalate without proper coping mechanisms. Many young people have limited experience in identifying and expressing how they feel, and when left unchecked, these emotional struggles can carry over into adulthood—potentially worsening existing mental health conditions.

"Having more guidance on emotional literacy in school or at home would have been beneficial. Learning specific strategies to identify and cope with emotions could have made it easier to navigate challenging feelings."

- Student Interview

Current Gaps

High Costs & Limited Access

Therapies and premium apps can be expensive or uncovered by insurance, putting consistent professional support out of reach for many families.

Lack of Child Engagement

Most solutions aren't tailored to younger users—complex interfaces and long sessions often fail to hold a child's interest, reducing long-term use.

Scheduling & Time Constraints

Busy family routines can make fixed therapy sessions difficult to attend, while digital options may require stable connectivity or additional resources.

Privacy & Oversight Concerns

Parents often need some visibility into their child's mental well-being, yet children want autonomy and confidentiality. Striking this balance can be challenging without well-designed safeguards.

Why Our Approach Is Different

Our goal is to empower children by helping them build emotional awareness and resilience before problems escalate. Unlike costly or highly specialized options, our solution:

  • Delivers Daily, Engaging Questionnaires: Short, fun, and thoughtful prompts drawn from inspirational sources that invite children to reflect on their day and emotions.
  • Offers a Consistent Routine: Simple reminders and journaling streaks encourage ongoing use, building healthy habits around self-expression.
  • Prioritizes Accessibility: By focusing on a digital journaling platform, we aim to make emotional support tools more widely available to youth, regardless of economic or geographic constraints.

By honing in on child-friendly prompts and regular self-reflection, our project tackles a critical gap in early mental health support—fostering emotional literacy in adolescence so that future generations can step into adulthood better equipped to handle life's pressures.
