Track 1: Podcast on Inspiring Children

Sponsor

Dr. Young, Department of Emergency Medicine and Informatics, School of Information and Computer Sciences

Parenting or teaching K-12 students often challenges the mental well-being of everyone involved. Building on Dr. Young's research and his book "Stick With It", this track aims to explore the psychology behind how to inspire children to cultivate lasting passions in different subjects.

Through user interviews and discussions with role-model kids, the team works to identify common struggles parents and teachers face, and to provide practical, research-backed strategies for boosting kids' engagement and motivation.

A key deliverable is a website introducing Dr. Young's upcoming podcast, which will showcase these insights and encourage healthier, more enthusiastic learning for K-12 youth.

Meet Our Team

This project was developed by Francis Luigi Lozano, Ann Bui, Asher Wu, and Harjot Jaswal at the University of California Irvine's Department of Informatics. Over an intensive 10-week period, our team collaborated to bring MoodMinder from concept to reality.

Our Mission

Our mission is to create an accessible, engaging, and cost-effective tool that empowers children to manage their emotions and build resilience through self-reflection and journaling. We believe that fostering early emotional awareness is key to improving long-term mental health outcomes.

Our Journey

Through extensive research including surveys and interviews with children, parents, and college students, we have designed a solution that not only addresses a critical gap in today's mental health support systems but also encourages daily practices for emotional growth.

Weeks 1-3: Research & Discovery

Conducted comprehensive literature review, user interviews, and market analysis to understand the current landscape of children's mental health solutions.

Weeks 4-6: Design & Iteration

Developed wireframes, user flows, and interactive prototypes, continuously refining our design based on user feedback and usability testing.

Weeks 7-8: Technical Implementation

Integrated AI capabilities, built core features, and established the foundation for a secure, child-friendly platform.

Weeks 9-10: Testing & Refinement

Conducted final user testing, made performance optimizations, and prepared comprehensive documentation for future development.

Team Roles

CEO (Team Lead)

Oversees the overall vision and strategy of the mental health journaling platform. Ensures the team stays aligned with the core mission of empowering children's emotional well-being through digital journaling. Manages key stakeholder relationships and provides high-level guidance on business decisions.

CTO (Technology Lead)

Heads the technical direction of the project. Responsible for selecting the tech stack, ensuring data security (especially crucial for children's mental health data), and maintaining platform reliability. Oversees backend and frontend development, system architecture, and integrations.

UX Lead (Prototype & User Experience)

Focuses on creating a child-friendly and intuitive user interface. Designs the journaling features, mood tracking elements, and overall navigation flow. Conducts user research, prototypes interfaces, and iterates based on feedback to ensure a smooth and engaging experience for children.

Team Lead (Project Coordination)

Manages day-to-day tasks, sprint planning, and team collaboration. Ensures deadlines are met, coordinates cross-functional communication, and supports the CEO in keeping the project on track. Helps address any roadblocks and fosters a collaborative environment.

Closing

By bringing together diverse expertise in design, development, research, and coordination, Track 1 teams aim to produce a comprehensive, research-driven solution that empowers children to better understand and manage their mental health.
